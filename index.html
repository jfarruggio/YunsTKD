ETIQUETTE & PROTOCOL

Structure and courtesy are important parts of the martial arts. Proper etiquette and protocol is necessary to teach these values.

  • The tenets of Tae Kwon Do and the Children's Home Rules are to be followed at all times.​​​
  • When entering or leaving the school, the student is to bow to the head instructor or the senior student.
  • Any social conversations should be conducted in the reception area or the locker rooms and should be done so in a quiet tone.
  • Conversations on the training mat should be confined to Tae Kwon Do and kept to a minimum.
  • Students in uniform are not allowed to sit on the chairs in the waiting area.
  • Regardless of your personal relationships, senior students should be addressed as Mister or Ms, Sir or Ma'am and should be answered yes sir and no sir or yes ma'am and no ma'am.
  • When stepping onto or off the mat, stop at the edge and salute the flags and bow to the senior student.
  • Unless there is an emergency, students are not to leave the mat area after the start of class without the teaching instructor's permission.
  • If you arrive late, change as quickly as possible and line up at the back of the class. If warm-up is over, wait for directions from the teaching instructor.
  • For your safety, ask permission to use pads and equipment, (other than the hanging bags) from an instructor and make sure other people are not in the way.
  • Do not spar with other students unless you have the permission and supervision of an instructor.
  • Instructors are allowed to correct students but NEVER are they allowed to insult, degrade, or humiliate the students.
  • If a student is corrected in a manner that they do not understand or agree with they should POLITELY discuss this with the instructor at the end of class.

    • TENENTS & OATH

    Tenets of Tae Kwon Do
    • Courtesy
    • Integrity
    • Perseverance
    • Self-control
    • Indomitable Spirit
    Student Oath
    • I shall observe the tenets of Tae Kwon Do.
    • I shall respect my instructors and seniors.
    • I shall never misuse Tae Kwon Do.
    • I shall be a champion of freedom and justice.
    • I shall build a more peaceful world.

    Children's Home Rules

    1. Children must show respect to their parents and family members at all times.​​
    2. Children shall greet their parents when they enter the house and tell them goodbye when they leave.
    3. Children will be truthful at all times.
    4. Children will maintain a good relationship with their brothers and sisters.
    5. Children must help with household chores.
    6. Children will keep their own rooms neat and clean.
    7. Children must keep their bodies, hair and teeth clean at all times.
    8. Children will not interrupt adult conversations.
    9. Children will study their school work at school and at home.
    10. Children must show respect for their teachers and peers at all times.
